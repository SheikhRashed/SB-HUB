FraudStop - if you don’t report it, you support it

Fraud has many faces and knows no boundaries. As such, the Bank relies on its employees to identify, report and expose incidents and suspected incidents of fraud because fraud prevention and detection is everyone’s responsibility.

  • What is fraud stop
  • Types of fraud

What is Fraud Stop

FraudStop is a reward programme for bank employees who report crime(s) relating to financial crimes including fraud, theft, bribery & corruption and collusion by employees of Standard Bank, clients, suppliers, business partners, and third parties. The programme is also instrumental in raising awareness on the Risks associated with fraud and has also assisted in creating a culture of vigilant employees who go above and beyond the call of duty to safeguard the assets and reputation of the organisation.

Cyber fraud (phishing and/or smishing scams) cases submitted are not eligible for FraudStop reward and should be reported to phishing@standardbank.co.za. Money laundering incidents should be reported through the Money laundering reporting process, and is also not eligable for FraudStop reward.​

Process for reporting fraud

Employees submit potential fraudulent incidents

  • - Rules when reporting fraud
  • - Submit fraud incident

The case is routed to a Fraud Analyst for further investigation. The investigation will be conducted based on merit and evidence submitted

  • - Track and trace incident

As soon as a conclusion is made, the submitter will be advised via email of the outcome, which could be “Confirmed fraud”, “Confirmed not fraud” or “Confirmed fraud but not eligible for reward”.

In some instances, certain cases might take longer to conclude than others, as the incident could be referred to a 3rd party such as the Department of Home Affairs or Group Investigations for further investigation, depending on the nature of the incident.

Fraud Stop Rules

  • Only one person can be rewarded per confirmed reported fraud incident.

  • All fraud detected must be captured within 7 days of the incident taking place.

  • A reward of R750 (before tax) or the local currency equivalent will be paid out to staff per confirmed Fraud incident reported.

    • R5 million or above per confirmed fraud case within CHNW.

    • R10 million or above per confirmed fraud case within CIB and CF.

  • Every month R50,000 (before tax) or the local currency equivalent will be paid to one staff member, from the pool of staff who reported confirmed fraud incidents.

    • This is done under the supervision and validation of external auditors to maintain fairness at all times

    • A staff member is eligible to win the R50,000 only once a year.

  • Once a year in December a R1 million draw will take place which consist of the following staff.

    • The 12 monthly R50,000 winners.

    • Staff who prevented fraud above the CHNW, CIB and CF thresholds.

  • Randomly drawn confirmed fraud entries from the entire year within CHNW, CIB, EF, Wealth & ROA staff member receiving an above threshold reward in two consecutive years will only be invited to the million rand draw in the first year of receiving the reward and not both.

  • The fraudstop platform allows you to upload the documents

  • Directors, and any staff directly involved with the administration of the programme will not be eligible for reward.

  • Staff members whose primary function is fraud prevention, i.e. where fraud prevention constitutes 50% or more of their KRAs do not qualify for FraudStop rewards, however are encouraged to still make reports.

  • All Refund / Reverse Deposit Fraud Scams and Counterfeit Notes will not qualify for a reward but must be reported.

  • Staff members, who are under resignation at the time of the draws, will not qualify for any reward.

  • Staff members, who have disciplinary action or any pending disciplinary action against them, will not qualify for any reward.

  • Cyber fraud (phishing scams) cases submitted will not be eligible for payment and should rather be reported to phishing@standardbank.co.za.

  • No reward is payable for Money laundering incidents loaded to FraudStop, the Money laundering reporting process must be followed.

Application Fraud

Where an applicant has used his or her own name but has made an application for some form of credit, account, policy or service which contains a material misrepresentation - for example, false employment details, false payslip, or false address.

Some examples of application fraud include:

  • Exaggerating income to qualify for a larger credit facility; or

  • Applying for a vehicle where the value of the vehicle is significantly overstated; or

  • Applying for a loan with a falsified bank statement from another bank​​​

Transaction Fraud

Transactional fraud can take many different forms and involves some form of misrepresentation. Transactional fraud involves the movement of money from an account to another and usually under false pretenses.

Examples of transactional fraud are as follows: :

  • 419 scams;

  • Change of banking details;

  • False deposits; or

  • Kite-flying.

Any financial transaction that was not authorised directly by the account holder is considered to be a fraudulent transaction​.​​​​

insurance Fraud

Occurs when any act is committed with the intent to fraudulently obtain some benefit or advantage to which they are not otherwise entitled or someone knowingly denies some benefit that is due and to which someone is entitled.

The most common schemes include:

  • Claiming for an item that was never owned;

  • Overstating damages on a claim;

  • Misrepresenting facts about an incident (e.g. lying in an affidavit or claiming that someone else was driving the vehicle at the time of the accident); or

  • Submitting false death certificates.

Any financial transaction that was not authorised directly by the account holder is considered to be a fraudulent transaction​.​​​​

staff Fraud

Occurs when an individual intentionally deceives their employer or their employer’s customer’s for personal gain (mostly financial but not always). Bribery and corruption are other forms of staff fraud and may result in unjustified benefit with severe repercussions for the employee and employer.

Some examples of staff fraud include:

  • Collusion with syndicates to defraud the bank/ customers;

  • Submitting fraudulent staff claims (e.g. Expenses, Leave);

Favouring a service provider because of payments received; or not declaring a direct conflict of interest (e.g. running a consulting business that supplies services directly to your department).​​​

Recognize it, Report it & get Rewarded!

Employees are encouraged to submit suspected instances of fraud online via the FraudStop programme.

Get rewarded with R750 for every submission that is confirmed fraud.

Automatically qualif​y for the monthly draw, and stand the chance to win a reward of R50,000.

  • A monthly winner is drawn randomly from a pool of the confirmed fraud cases and is eligible for a R50 000 (before tax) reward.

  • The draw is conducted under the supervision of an independent, external auditor.

Employees who report suspicious cases above the set thresholds cases will be automatically entered into the annual R500 000 draw: The thresholds are as follows:

  • R5 million or above per confirmed case (Consumer and High Net Worth).

  • R10 million or above per confirmed case (Corporate and Investment Banking and Corporate Functions).

  • Each of these cases will also receive an additional reward of R5 000 per case.

An Annual Draw is hosted by Integrated Operational Risk in November, where 30 lucky finalists compete for the grand prize of a Million Rands!
